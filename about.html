  • About Me

    My name is Justin Cook and I am currently in my final year of studies at the University of Toronto Institute of Aerospace Studies, where I'm completing a Master's of Engineering in Aerospace Engineering with an Emphasis on Robotics. Previously, I completed my Bachelor of Engineering in Mechatronics at McMaster University. I chose to pursue Mechatronics Engineering and then a Master's of Engineering due to my long-standing interest - from age 4 - in the field of robotics. My interest in robotics along with the Institute of Aerospace Studies excellent Robotics offering lead me to choose to complete my Master's there. This early interest in robotics inspired my education choices and lead me to work on several robots, such as Sumobot at McMaster, over the years as both hobby and extracurricular projects, along with work on robots for classes. Currently, I am applying my controls knowledge from robotics to help design the ADCS subsystem for a CubeSat on the University of Toronto Aerospace Team.

  • My Interests

    Other than my keen interest in robotics, I have delved into other and similar fields along with various hobbies. In fields related to robotics, I have enjoyed learning to create desktop scripts and web applications and sites, such as this one, along with, of course, building my own robots as hobbies and projects. Throughout my life I have also loved to use my spare time to read fantasy and science fiction novels, play hockey and soccer, learn martial arts and boxing, and bike.
